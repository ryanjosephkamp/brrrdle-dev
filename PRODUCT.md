# Product

## Register

product

## Users

brrrdle serves casual and committed word-game players using desktop and mobile browsers. Players may play as guests, sign in to retain account-scoped progress, return to unfinished Solo games, compete asynchronously with other signed-in players, inspect results and definitions, and review their history, progression, and ranking information.

The primary job is simple: enter a game quickly, understand its state immediately, submit guesses without interface friction, and trust that progress and results will remain correct across navigation, refresh, sign-in, and device changes.

## Product Purpose

brrrdle combines classic single-puzzle OG play with five-puzzle GO chains across Daily, Practice, and authenticated Multiplayer experiences. Success means the game remains fully playable, responsive, accessible, and trustworthy while supporting durable persistence, clear progression, privacy-safe public identity, and real multiplayer synchronization.

The functional shell is a neutral foundation for later frontend work. It must retain the complete product and backend behavior while reducing decorative weight, rendering cost, and visual inconsistency.

## Brand Personality

Focused, dependable, playful.

The interface should feel like a well-made game tool: direct enough for repeated use, clear enough for a first-time player, and lively only when feedback helps the player understand what happened.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated dashboard styling, especially glass panels, ambient glows, excessive gradients, decorative shadows, and repeated all-caps eyebrow labels.
- Slot-machine-like visual noise, constant motion, or effects that compete with gameplay.
- Marketing-page composition inside operational game surfaces.
- Mystery controls, nonstandard affordances, nested cards, and presentation that obscures state.
- A visual simplification that removes features, weakens accessibility, or makes gameplay feedback ambiguous.

## Design Principles

1. Gameplay state is always clearer than decoration.
2. Preserve trust: progress, identity, results, and multiplayer state must remain stable and understandable.
3. Familiar controls should disappear into the task instead of drawing attention to themselves.
4. Mobile play is a first-class workflow, not a compressed desktop afterthought.
5. Motion and sound communicate state; neither exists merely to decorate a page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Preserve semantic landmarks, headings, forms, labels, dialogs, tabs, status regions, and keyboard operation.
- Maintain visible focus and sufficient text/control contrast.
- Do not rely on color alone for important game or status meaning.
- Honor reduced-motion preferences and keep essential feedback perceivable without animation.
- Prevent horizontal overflow, clipped menus, and unreachable controls at narrow mobile widths.
